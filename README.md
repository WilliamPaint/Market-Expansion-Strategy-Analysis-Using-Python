<div style="background:#f3f4f6;padding:8px 12px;border-left:5px solid #6366f1;margin:18px 0 10px 0;">
  <strong>Overview</strong>
</div>
<p>
  This project analyzes sales, profit, quantity, and cost data across U.S. regions, states,
  and product categories to guide a market expansion strategy. Using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib),
  the analysis identifies profitable regions and categories, flags underperforming markets,
  and translates quantitative findings into actionable recommendations for sustainable growth.
</p>

<div style="background:#f3f4f6;padding:8px 12px;border-left:5px solid #eab308;margin:18px 0 10px 0;">
  <strong>Objectives</strong>
</div>
<ul>
  <li>Determine which regions consistently generate high sales and profit.</li>
  <li>Identify untapped markets with potential for expansion.</li>
  <li>Analyze product performance by category/subcategory to guide inventory and marketing.</li>
  <li>Use profitability and cost trends to support data-driven decision-making.</li>
</ul>

<div style="background:#f3f4f6;padding:8px 12px;border-left:5px solid #16a34a;margin:18px 0 10px 0;">
  <strong>Methodology</strong>
</div>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Data Cleaning &amp; Preparation:</strong> Worked with 9,994 transactions and 20 variables; retained meaningful outliers to preserve insights.</li>
  <li><strong>Descriptive Analysis:</strong> Generated summary statistics for sales, profit, discount, and CoGS.</li>
  <li><strong>Correlation Analysis:</strong> Built a correlation matrix showing strong relationships:
    <ul>
      <li>Sales–CoGS = <strong>0.928</strong></li>
      <li>Sales–Profit = <strong>0.479</strong></li>
      <li>Discount–Profit = <strong>-0.219</strong></li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li><strong>Visualization:</strong> Produced bar charts, correlation heatmaps, and region/state comparisons to uncover patterns.</li>
  <li><strong>Strategic Interpretation:</strong> Converted findings into business recommendations (e.g., expand in South, control costs in Central, reinforce West/East).</li>
</ul>

<div style="background:#f3f4f6;padding:8px 12px;border-left:5px solid #db2777;margin:18px 0 10px 0;">
  <strong>Key Findings</strong>
</div>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Top Regions:</strong> West and East are most profitable, with West leading in both sales and profit.</li>
  <li><strong>Expansion Opportunity:</strong> The South underperforms, offering the greatest potential for targeted growth.</li>
  <li><strong>Product Insights:</strong> Technology yields the highest profit; Office Supplies drive demand; Furniture lags due to high costs.</li>
  <li><strong>State-Level Insights:</strong> California dominates sales and profit; states like Texas and Pennsylvania generate losses.</li>
  <li><strong>Trend Analysis:</strong> Sales rise year-over-year, but profit growth lags due to rising costs and discount pressure.</li>
</ul>

<div style="background:#f3f4f6;padding:8px 12px;border-left:5px solid #22c55e;margin:18px 0 10px 0;">
  <strong>Impact</strong>
</div>
<p>
  The analysis provides a data-driven roadmap for expansion: prioritize the South, address
  inefficiencies in Central, and replicate success strategies from West and East. The methodology
  is transferable beyond retail to market entry and growth strategy problems across industries.
</p>
